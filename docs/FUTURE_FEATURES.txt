FEATURE: Base Plate Assembly (Fig 4D, 4H)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Priority: Medium
Status: PLANNED
Description:
  - Flat rectangular base plate (shown in Fig 4H)
  - Provides mounting surface for A-frame stands
  - Material visualization: aluminum/steel appearance
  - Dimensions should be proportional to outer frame (~2x outer frame width)
CAD Reference: Fig 4D (Base Sub-Assembly), Fig 4H (CAD of Base Plate)
Notes:
  - Consider adding rubber feet/mounting holes for realism


FEATURE: A-Frame Support Stands (Fig 4D, 4E)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Priority: Medium  
Status: PLANNED
Description:
  - Two triangular A-frame stands on opposite sides of base plate
  - Support the outer frame via bearings at top
  - Angled legs for stability
  - Motor mount points on top of A-frames
CAD Reference: Fig 4D (Base Sub-Assembly), Fig 4E (Model of Stand)
Notes:
  - These hold the outer motor and outer frame vertical shafts


FEATURE: Motor Housing/Mounts
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Priority: Low
Status: PLANNED
Description:
  - Visible motor housings on A-frame tops (outer motor)
  - Motor housing on outer frame for inner motor
  - NEMA stepper motor representation
  - Belt/coupling visualization (optional)
Notes:
  - Consider adding motor specs display (NEMA 17/23/34)


FEATURE: Bearing Housings
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Priority: Low
Status: PLANNED
Description:
  - Cylindrical bearing housings at rotation pivot points
  - Top/bottom of outer frame vertical shafts
  - Left/right of inner frame horizontal shafts
  - Flanged bearing appearance
Notes:
  - Small detail but adds mechanical realism


================================================================================
                              CONNECTION GEOMETRY
================================================================================

FEATURE: Outer Frame Vertical Shafts (Fig 4F, 5A)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Priority: HIGH
Status: IMPLEMENTED - v3.1.0
Description:
  - Vertical cylindrical shafts extending from top and bottom of outer frame
  - These are the rotation axis for outer motor (ψ rotation around Y-axis)
  - Connect outer frame to A-frame stands via bearings
CAD Reference: Fig 4F (Outer Frame Sub-Assembly), Fig 5A
Implementation Notes:
  - Use THREE.CylinderGeometry for shafts
  - Position at center-top and center-bottom of outer frame
  - Shaft length ~15-20% of outer frame height


FEATURE: Inner-to-Outer Frame Horizontal Bars (Fig 4I, 5C)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Priority: HIGH
Status: IMPLEMENTED - v3.1.0
Description:
  - Two horizontal cylindrical bars connecting inner frame to outer frame
  - These are the rotation axis for inner motor (φ rotation around Z-axis)
  - Positioned on left and right sides of inner frame
CAD Reference: Fig 4I (Inner Frame Sub-Assembly), Fig 5C
Implementation Notes:
  - Use THREE.CylinderGeometry rotated 90° (along Z-axis)
  - Connect from inner frame edge to outer frame inner edge
  - Bar diameter proportional to frame size
